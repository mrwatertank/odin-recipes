Idli Recipe



About Idli Recipe

Idli is a traditional South Indian steamed food made from fermented rice and urad dal
batter. The batter is ground and fermented before steaming.

The batter is poured into moulds and steamed until soft. Idli is commonly eaten for
breakfast. It is served with coconut chutney and sambar.

  • Prep Time: 9 hours

  • Cook Time: 25 minutes

  • Total Time: 9 hours 25 minutes

  • Serving Size: 30 Idlis

  • Course: Breakfast, Snacks

  • Cuisine: South Indian

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup regular rice + 1 cup parboiled rice or 2 cups idli rice or 2 cups parboiled rice

  • ½ cup (120 grams) urad dal ‐ whole or split (husked black gram)

  • ¼ cup (20 grams) thick poha (flattened rice)

  • ¼ teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)

  • 2 cups water ‐ for soaking rice

  • 1 cup water ‐ for soaking urad dal

  • ½ cup water ‐ for grinding urad dal or add as required

  • ¾ to 1 cup water ‐ for grinding rice or add as required

  • 1 teaspoon rock salt (edible and food grade) or sea salt

  • oil ‐ as required to apply to the idli moulds

  • 2 to 2.5 cups water ‐ for steaming

INSTRUCTIONS

Soaking rice and lentils

  1. Pick and rinse both the regular rice and parboiled rice.

  2. Rinse the poha and add to the rice.

  3. Add water. Mix well. Cover and keep the rice and poha to soak for 4 to 5 hours.

  4. In a separate bowl, rinse the urad dal and methi seeds a couple of times.

  5. Soak the urad dal with methi seeds separately in water for 4 to 5 hours.

Making Idli Batter

  1. Drain the soaked urad dal. Reserve the water.

  2. First, grind the urad dal and methi seeds with ¼ cup of the reserved water
    for some seconds. Then add another ¼ cup and continue grinding until
    the batter is smooth, fluffy, and light.

  3. Transfer the urad dal batter to a bowl.

  4. Drain and grind the soaked rice and poha with water in batches to a smooth,
    slightly grainy consistency.

  5. Mix both the batters together in a large bowl or pan. Add salt and mix well
    using clean hands or a spatula.

  6. Cover and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8 to 12 hours or until
    it doubles in volume.

Making Idli

  1. Before you begin steaming, heat 2 to 2.5 cups water in an idli steamer, or
    a pot or pressure cooker until the water comes to a light boil or simmer.

  2. Grease or brush the idli moulds lightly with oil.

  3. Gently stir the fermented batter. Pour it into the moulds.

  4. Once the water in the idli steamer, pot or pressure cooker comes to a light
    boil, place the idli mould stand.

  5. Steam in a steamer or pressure cooker (without the vent weight/whistle) for
    12 to 15 minutes, or until the batter is cooked firm.

  6. To check doneness, insert a toothpick and it should come out clean. The
    surface should look set and feel springy.

  7. Let the idlis rest for 2 to 3 minutes, then gently remove with a spoon.

Serving and Storage

  1. Serve the steaming hot idli with coconut chutney and sambar.

  2. Store leftover idli batter in the fridge for 3 to 4 days. Idlis can be
    refrigerated for up to 2 days and reheated by steaming or microwaving
    with a sprinkle of water.
